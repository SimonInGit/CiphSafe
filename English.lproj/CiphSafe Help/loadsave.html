CiphSafe Loading, Saving, and Changing the Passphrase

Loading

Loading an existing document will cause an 'Enter Passphrase' window, in which (obviously) you enter the passphrase for that document. If the passphrase doesn't work, the window will return, giving another opportunity. You may continue this until you enter the correct passphrase or hit the cancel button. If the passphrase has been forgotten, all data in the document is lost.

Saving

The first time a document is saved, a sheet will attach to the document's window asking first for the name of the new document. After it is named, a new sheet will appear asking for the passphrase used to protect the document. There are two fields in which to enter the passphrase, to make sure you've entered the correct phrase. Choosing a passphrase is important as a short, simple one (eg, "my passphrase") makes for weak protection, and a long complicated one (eg, "seven spry dobermans ran across the rickety old bridge after midnight") is quite safe (except for that specific example now that it's been documented).

Changing the passphrase

Anytime after a document has been saved you can change the passphrase protecting it (via File->Change Passphrase). Just like saving, it requires you to enter it twice to confirm. Once the passphrase is changed, the document will be saved immediately with the new phrase.
